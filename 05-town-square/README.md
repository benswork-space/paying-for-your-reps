# Town Square — Hyperlocal Civic Social Network

## Concept
A location-based feed of civic activity in your area: upcoming city council meetings, local ballot measures, what your neighbors are organizing around, who's running for school board. Not a news aggregator — a participation platform.

## Why This Matters
- Local government is where policy most directly affects daily life
- Local races are won by dozens of votes; local organizing has outsized impact
- Most people feel alone in their frustration; seeing neighbors engaged is powerful

## Key Features
- Location-based civic activity feed
- City council / school board meeting calendar + agendas
- Local ballot measure explanations
- Neighbor activity ("3 people near you are going to Tuesday's zoning hearing")
- Local candidate profiles and forums

## Data Sources
- Local government websites (scraping — no standardized APIs exist)
- Crowdsourced event and meeting data
- State/county election office data

## Key Challenges
- **Cold-start problem** — social products need users to be useful, need to be useful to get users
- **Local data** — the hardest civic data to obtain; no standardization across 90,000+ local governments
- **Moderation** — location-based social platforms attract bad actors
- **Sustainability** — high operational cost relative to revenue potential

## Status
Priority #5 — Long-term concept. Hardest to execute due to cold-start and data challenges. Revisit after other projects establish a user base.
